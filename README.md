KEY FINDINGS: 

Using a Window Function, the data suggests that the quality of teachers is not a "catch all" factor. In public schools a good teacher improves student's exam scores 
with a 1.14 performance gap between high and low quality teachers and in the private sector it is much lower at 0.40.

On the interesting part, motivation levels barely had any impact on exams scores suggesting that most of the heavy lifting comes to being consistent and doing
your due diligence. Additionally looking at the data, while making sure you are healthy and well rested would imply an increase in motivation, grades and more
it did not significantly affect the students performance.

Overall what it came down to affecting the exam scores was being consistent and attentive by showing up to class. There was a direct postiive correlation between 
attendance and exam scores going up. As you attended more classes the higher mark you got in the exams.
